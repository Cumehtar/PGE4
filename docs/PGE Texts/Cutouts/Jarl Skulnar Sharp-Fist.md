Jarl1 [[Skulnar Sharp-Fist]] is known as the founder of the Skulnarssons of [[Falkreath]], which have ruled the Earldom since the end of the [[Second War of the Lake]] in 4E 267, a nigh-unparalleled line of descent among the myriad nobility of Wrothgaria.

As best as can be ascertained from the scant historical records of Falkreath, Skulnar began his ascent to power as a housecarl2 in the service of Jarl [[Jorlief Jurgensen]]. Skulnar, unusually for the people of Falkreath then, and indeed now, was literate, resulting in him being granted the position of Steward of the Court, a position that became of increasing importance as Jorlief attempted to reclaim and expand the historical Falkreath Hold. Following Jorlief’s successes in the [[First War of the Lake]], Skulnar was put in charge of organizing and distributing the reclaimed land among the nobility of the fyrd, as well as serving as a broker and mediator as claims inevitably clashed. Skulnar himself remained landless, holding little to his name save for his arms, armor, and steed, a state of poverty that seems to have aided in his mediations, as none of the squabbling nobility could accuse him of personal bias.3

Skulnar appears to have spent the interwar years in much the same role, bookkeeper, broker, and occasional bodyguard to his liege lord, only completing his ascent to power a decade later, amidst the disastrous Second War of the Lake. Widespread uprisings in reconquered territory, supported by Snow-Throat’s militias and equipped with alchemical weaponry, first drove the Wrothgarian nobility from their lands and then surged into Falkreath Hold itself, threatening the forts of the Legion Road and the city itself. Jarl Jorlief received what was ultimately a fatal wound in the fighting, surviving only long enough to deem his loyal housecarl and steward his successor. Skulnar humbly accepted the burden of authority, brokering a peace with the [[Ilinalta]]n Moot to end the war.4

Historians have sometimes suggested that Skulnar’s reign sowed the seeds of Falkreath’s pivot from the Wrothgarian economy of honor and oaths to a more conventional one of coin and debt. While it is true that Skulnar’s descendants have been at the forefront of such a transition, Skulnar himself appears to have been thoroughly immersed in Wrothgarian customs, eschewing gold and gems as means of trade. Whatever the truth, Skulnar’s line has ruled Falkreath stably and faithfully in the years since his death, guiding the Hold through word and action alike.

4. _Rumors persist that Skulnar himself was the cause of Jorlief’s death, and had a hand in instigating the uprisings._
